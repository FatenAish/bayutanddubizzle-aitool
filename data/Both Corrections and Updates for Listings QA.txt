Bayut & dubizzle — Corrections and Updates for Listings and Projects (Q&A)
Q) What does correction needed in listings mean?

A:

Verify and correct data related to property listings, developers, and projects across both Bayut and dubizzle platforms.

Ensure all updates are factually accurate, aligned with brand guidelines, and consistent across English and Arabic versions.

Facilitate smooth communication and coordination with the Operations Team for technical updates or backend changes.

Maintain documentation and tracking of all edits, requests, and resolutions.

Q) How does the Content Team submit correction requests?

A: Through the established Slack channel, including supporting details such as URLs, screenshots, or references for each request.

Q) What must be included in each correction request?

A:

Link to listing or project

Description of the issue

Required correction (English / Arabic)

Source or reference (official website, Bayut database, etc.)

Q) What does the Content Team need to validate before submitting?

A: Factual accuracy such as developer names, locations, payment plans, handover dates, etc.

Q5) What does the Operations Team do?

A:

Execute approved corrections and updates within the backend system.

Ensure data synchronization across English and Arabic listings.

Notify the Content Team once the update is completed for verification.

Q) Who is the Channel Handler (Coordination Lead) and what do they do?

A: Faten Aish is the Channel Handler. They:

Serve as the main liaison between the Content and Operations teams.

Review incoming requests to ensure completeness and accuracy before escalation.

Track all requests, monitor progress, and follow up on pending actions.

Maintain an updated log of completed, ongoing, and rejected requests.

Q) What happens during the request submission step?

A: Content teams share names, listings, or locations requiring edits through the shared channel.

Q) What happens during the initial review step?

A: The channel handler reviews requests for completeness and verifies data before sending to Operations. Incomplete or unclear requests are returned for clarification.

Q) What happens during Operations implementation?

A: Operations updates the listings, project details, or metadata based on the approved correction request.

Q) What happens during QA?

A: After updates are completed, the channel handler verifies the changes live on the platform. If discrepancies persist, a follow-up correction request is submitted.

Q) How are requests closed and tracked?

A: Each completed request is logged in the tracking sheet with timestamps and status. Periodic audits are conducted to ensure data remains accurate and consistent.
