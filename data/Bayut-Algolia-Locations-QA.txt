Bayut Algolia Locations – Q&A

Q: What are Algolia Locations?
A: Algolia Locations are tags added to blog articles to associate content with specific geographic areas, helping improve search accuracy and location-based content discovery.

Q: What is the purpose of Algolia Locations?
A: The purpose is to ensure all published articles have accurate location tags so they are searchable, properly indexed, and ready for migration to the Strapi system. fileciteturn3file0

Q: Which articles require Algolia Locations?
A:
- All published articles in WordPress
- Both new and existing articles that reference a specific emirate or area

Q: Which articles do NOT require Algolia Locations?
A:
- Unpublished or draft articles
- Articles outside WordPress (test or training)
- Discussion articles or content covering the entire UAE without a specific location 

Q: Who is responsible for Algolia Locations?
A:
- Sub-editor: ensures every article has an Algolia Location and updates the tracking sheet
- Content Team: adds locations to newly published articles

Q: How often are Algolia Locations reviewed?
A: Algolia Locations are reviewed weekly, every Sunday. 

Q: How do we add or verify Algolia Locations?
A:
1. Open WordPress and launch the Algolia Locations Tool
2. Open the Articles Algolia Locations Google Sheet
3. Add newly published articles to the sheet
4. Verify that the correct location is added
5. If missing, manually add the location using the Algolia tool
6. If the location does not exist, raise a request through Product or Tech

Q: How are updated articles reviewed?
A:
1. Review all articles updated during the week
2. Ensure each article includes an Algolia Location
3. Confirm each article is listed in the Google Sheet
4. Escalate repeated omissions to the line manager

Q: What tools are used for Algolia Locations?
A:
- Algolia Locations Tool (for adding and verifying locations)
- Articles Algolia Locations Google Sheet (for tracking)
- WordPress Dashboard (for article updates)
