Corrections and Updates for Listings – Q&A

Q: What is the purpose of corrections and updates for listings?
A: The purpose is to ensure property listings and project information on Bayut and dubizzle are accurate, consistent, and compliant with brand standards, while coordinating smoothly with the Operations team for implementation. 

Q: What does this process cover?
A:
- Property listings, developers, and projects on Bayut and dubizzle
- English and Arabic content consistency
- Verification, correction, and tracking of updates 

Q: Who submits correction requests?
A: The Content Teams at Bayut and dubizzle submit correction requests through the designated Slack channel, ensuring all information is factually accurate before submission. fileciteturn6file0

Q: What information must be included in a correction request?
A:
- Link to the listing or project
- Description of the issue
- Required correction (English and/or Arabic)
- Source or reference (official website, Bayut database, screenshots, etc.)

Q: Who reviews and coordinates correction requests?
A: The Coordination Lead (Channel Handler) reviews requests for completeness and accuracy, coordinates with Operations, tracks progress, and follows up on pending actions. 

Q: Who implements the corrections?
A: The Operations Team implements approved corrections in the backend system and ensures data synchronization across English and Arabic listings.

Q: Who verifies completed corrections?
A: Once updates are completed, the Channel Handler performs Quality Assurance (QA) by verifying the changes live on the platform and submits follow-ups if discrepancies remain. 

Q: How does the correction workflow work step by step?
A:
1. Content Team submits a correction request in the shared Slack channel with all required details
2. Channel Handler reviews and validates the request
3. Approved requests are escalated to the Operations Team
4. Operations Team applies the correction in the system
5. Channel Handler verifies the update live (QA)
6. The request is logged and closed in the tracking sheet

Q: How are correction requests tracked?
A: All requests are logged with status and timestamps in a tracking sheet, allowing visibility into completed, ongoing, and rejected requests. 

Q: How often are corrections handled?
A: Corrections are handled on an ongoing basis, with volume varying daily. Average review time is 10–15 minutes, though complex cases may take longer.
